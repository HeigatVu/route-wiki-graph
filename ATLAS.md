## Automated Linguistic Analysis
A 2024 systematic review ([[Shakeri-2024-nlp-ad-review]]) categorizes [[NLP]] methodologies into [[Sparse Text Representation]], [[Static Dense Text Representation]], and [[Contextualized Dense Text Representation]]. Earlier foundational reviews ([[Boschi-2017-connected-speech-review]]) emphasize that [[Linguistic Biomarkers]] should be categorized across five levels: Phonetic-phonological, [[Lexico-semantic]], Morpho-syntactic, [[Syntactic]], and [[Discourse-pragmatic]]. These markers map to specific clinical profiles; for instance, [[Alzheimer's Disease]] is primarily marked by [[Lexico-semantic]] and [[Discourse-pragmatic]] decline, whereas [[Primary Progressive Aphasia]] variants show distinct phonological or syntactic impairments. Standardized tools like [[CLAN]] are frequently used to extract these markers, though the choice of elicitation task—such as the [[Picture Description Task]] versus [[Story Narration Task]]—significantly influences the linguistic features captured.
